# RelayPay – Policies & Compliance

This document outlines RelayPay's company-level policies and compliance obligations in detail. It represents internal policy documentation used by customer support, operations, and compliance teams to understand regulatory requirements, security boundaries, and operational constraints.

This document defines what RelayPay as a business is allowed and not allowed to do. It is intentionally conservative and risk-aware. When policies apply, they override product convenience or customer expectations.

---

## 1. Regulatory & Compliance Framework

RelayPay operates in regulated financial environments across multiple jurisdictions, including Africa, Europe, and North America. As a financial services provider, RelayPay is required to comply with a range of local and international regulations related to:

- Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
- Counter-Terrorist Financing (CTF)
- Know Your Customer (KYC) and Know Your Business (KYB)
- Data protection and privacy regulations

Compliance obligations may differ depending on:

- User location
- Transaction corridor
- Transaction size, frequency, and risk profile

Compliance decisions are determined by automated systems and internal compliance teams. These decisions cannot be overridden by customer support or operations staff.

---

## 2. Identity Verification (KYC / KYB)

All RelayPay customers are required to complete identity verification before accessing full platform functionality.

Verification requirements may include:

- Business registration documents
- Proof of identity for directors, owners, or authorised signatories
- Proof of address or operating location

Verification timelines vary depending on jurisdiction, document quality, and review workload. In some cases, additional documentation may be requested after an account has already been approved.

RelayPay does not guarantee verification completion times. Verification outcomes and requests are determined by compliance requirements, not customer support discretion.

---

## 3. Transaction Monitoring & Risk Reviews

All transactions processed through RelayPay are subject to continuous monitoring.

Transactions may be:

- Automatically approved
- Delayed for review
- Temporarily held pending additional checks

Common triggers for review include:

- Unusual transaction patterns
- Large transaction amounts
- Changes to beneficiary details
- Activity inconsistent with historical usage

The specific rules and thresholds used for monitoring are confidential. RelayPay does not disclose internal risk logic or review criteria.

---

## 4. Account Restrictions & Suspensions

RelayPay may apply temporary restrictions or suspensions to accounts when:

- Required verification steps are incomplete
- Compliance or risk reviews are ongoing
- Suspicious or inconsistent activity is detected

Restrictions are precautionary measures and do not imply wrongdoing. In most cases, restrictions are lifted once required reviews or actions are completed.

Details about why a specific account was restricted are not always shareable due to regulatory and security considerations.

---

## 5. Data Security & Privacy

RelayPay is committed to protecting customer data and financial information.

Key security principles include:

- Least-privilege access to internal systems
- Segmentation of customer data
- Secure storage and transmission of sensitive information

Automated systems and customer-facing tools do not have access to:

- Account balances
- Transaction identifiers
- Personal identity documents
- Internal risk assessments

RelayPay does not share sensitive account information through automated or voice-based systems.

---

## 6. Disputes, Refunds & Cancellations

Disputes, refunds, and cancellations require manual handling by RelayPay's support and operations teams.

These processes may involve:

- Reviewing transaction records
- Coordinating with banking partners
- Compliance checks

RelayPay does not guarantee dispute outcomes or resolution timelines. Refunds and cancellations are evaluated on a case-by-case basis.

---

## 7. Communications & Representations

RelayPay communications must:

- Avoid guarantees or promises
- Accurately reflect policy and compliance limitations
- Avoid speculation or assumptions

RelayPay does not:

- Provide legal, tax, or financial advice
- Disclose internal decision-making logic
- Commit to specific outcomes or timelines

---

## Final Note

These policies exist to protect RelayPay, its customers, and its banking partners. When there is uncertainty or conflict between customer expectations and compliance obligations, RelayPay policy takes precedence.
